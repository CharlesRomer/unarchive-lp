The Process

Seven stages.
One complete manuscript.

01
Intake & Orientation

You share your calling, your audience, and the core idea you've been carrying. We listen before we write.

02
Voice Calibration

Upload sermons, articles, past writing, or simply talk to the system. Unarchive builds a precise model of how you sound — your cadence, your vocabulary, your rhetorical patterns.

03
Theological Anchoring

You define your non-negotiables — the convictions, frameworks, and scriptural lens that everything runs through. The system holds these as guardrails throughout the entire project.

04
Book Architecture

Working together, we build the structure: part divisions, chapter arc, tension and resolution, the logical throughline. You approve every decision before we draft a word.

05
Chapter Drafting

The core of the work. Each chapter is drafted in sessions — you direct, the system writes, you edit. The output sounds like you because it was built on you from the beginning.

06
Developmental Edit

Unarchive reviews the complete manuscript for pacing, consistency, argument strength, and voice fidelity — flagging every place where the book could be stronger.

07
Publish Readiness

Final formatting, chapter summaries, back-cover copy, and a market positioning brief. You leave with a manuscript ready for an agent, a publisher, or self-publishing — your call.

Questions

What people ask
before they begin.

Will it actually sound like me, or will it sound like AI? +

The Voice Engine is trained specifically on your material — your sermons, your articles, your past writing, even recordings of you speaking. It builds a model of how you construct sentences, which words you reach for, how you move between ideas. The result is prose that reads as yours because it was built on yours. You will still edit and direct — we recommend it — but the raw output will not feel generic.

How does Theology Lock work exactly? +

At the start of your project, you fill in a Theology Map — your core convictions, your non-negotiables, your hermeneutical approach, and any positions you hold that AI systems sometimes get wrong or flatten. These become permanent context that every generation pass is evaluated against. The system flags content that drifts from your stated framework and will not proceed past a flagged section without your review and approval.

What if I've never written a book before? +

Unarchive was designed for people who have never written a book before. The Book Architect walks you through structure step by step. The Chapter Writer uses conversation — you talk, it writes. You don't need to know how to construct a chapter; you need to know what you want to say. We help with everything else.

How long does it actually take? +

Most authors complete a full manuscript — 40,000 to 60,000 words — within 6 to 10 weeks of consistent sessions. The pace is entirely yours. You can work in focused sprints or steady daily sessions. The 3-month access window gives you space to draft, revise, and finalize without pressure.

Is this really my book, or is it the AI's book? +

It is your book. Every idea, every conviction, every story is yours. Unarchive is a tool — the same way a ghostwriter, a dictation service, or an editor is a tool. The content originates from you. The system helps you get it onto the page in a form that is publishable. You retain full ownership of everything produced.

What happens after 3 months if I'm not done? +

You can extend your access at a reduced rate. Most authors finish well within the 3-month window, but life happens. We don't abandon projects in progress. Contact support and we'll work something out.
